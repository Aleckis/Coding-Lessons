Dr. Norman Bourlag: A man who saved a BILLION lives

Time Line

  • 1914 - Born March 25 in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1937 - Earned B.S. in Forestry from University of Minnesota
  • 1939 - Completed M.S. in Plant Pathology from University of Minnesota
  • 1942 - Obtained Ph.D. in Plant Pathology from University of Minnesota
  • 1944-1960 - Worked in Mexico on disease-resistant wheat varieties
  • 1960s-1970s - Led wheat improvement programs in Asia and Africa
  • 1970 - Won Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1977 - Awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 1984-2009 - Served as Distinguished Professor at Texas A&M University
  • 1986 - Established World Food Prize
  • 2007 - Received Congressional Gold Medal
  • 2009 - Passed away on September 12 in Dallas, Texas
